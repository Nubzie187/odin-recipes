Country Ham and Biscuits

We admit it—we love the way that butter or some sort of grease makes food taste, especially bacon grease. That's why we use it to cook up country ham. I use it more than Rachel, so I have people save their bacon grease for me. I tell them, "Don't throw away your bacon grease!" And when somebody says, "How do you keep your skin looking so good?" I tell them, "Bacon grease...from the inside out!" — Dolly Parton

  • 1/4 cup shortening, plus more for greasing
  • 3 cups flour, plus more for dusting
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/2 cup cold butter, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1 1/4 cups whole buttermilk
  • 1 teaspoon bacon grease or shortening
  • 3 slices country ham
  • softened butter, for serving

Step by Step

  1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C). Grease an 8-inch cast-iron skillet with shortening.
  2. In a large mixing bowl or food processor, sift together flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and cream of tartar. Mix to combine.
  3. Cut butter and 1/4 cup shortening into the flour mixture until crumbly. Add buttermilk and gently stir until just combined.
  4. Turn out dough onto a floured surface. Pat to 2-inch thickness, cut out biscuits, and transfer to skillet. Bake for about 30 minutes.
  5. While baking, cook ham in a skillet with bacon grease until browned. Drain on a paper towel-lined plate.
  6. To serve, split biscuits, spread with butter, and add ham slices.
